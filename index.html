About the Sun Pharma Science Foundation (SPSF)

The Sun Pharma Science Foundation (SPSF) is dedicated to advancing science and healthcare in India by recognizing excellence in medical and pharmaceutical research, encouraging young scientists, and fostering scientific exchange. Through symposia, awards, and collaborative initiatives, SPSF has been at the forefront of promoting knowledge that drives innovation and aimproves patient care.

About The Symposium


Autoimmune diseases represent a significant global health challenge, affecting millions of people and leading to chronic conditions with profound personal, social, and economic consequences. This symposium will bring together leading researchers, clinicians, and young scientists to highlight the latest advancements in the field and foster collaborative innovation. This pivotal event is organized in proud collaboration with KIIT Deemed to be University. Under the esteemed patronage of Prof. (Dr.) Achyuta Samanta, Founder of KIIT, KISS & KIMS, the symposium is guided by the expertise of Organizing Chairmen Prof. G.C. Kundu (Director R&D, KIIT) and Prof. B. Ravindran (CEO, Imgenex).

Significance & Focus Areas

  • Chronic Conditions: Impact and potential for improved treatments.
  • Immune System Insights: Relevance to broader medical applications.
  • Genetics & Environment: Interplay in autoimmune diseases.
  • Targeted Therapies: Latest advancements.
  • Public Health Impact: Economic and societal implications.
  • Scientific Breakthroughs: Cross-disciplinary advancements.

Invited Speakers

Meet our esteemed panel of experts and researchers.

Dr. Soumen Basak

NII, New Delhi

Dr. Soumen Basak is a leading immunologist and head of the Systems Immunology Laboratory at the National Institute of Immunology (NII) in New Delhi, where he investigates the crosstalk between signal-activated intracellular pathways like canonical and non-canonical NF-κB signaling using biochemistry, genetics, and computational modeling. His work focuses on the role of these mechanisms in inflammation-related diseases such as cancer, inflammatory bowel diseases, and antiviral immunity against viruses like Chandipura and Chikungunya. A recipient of the prestigious Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize in Biological Sciences (2019) and fellowships from the Indian National Science Academy (2022), Indian Academy of Sciences (2021), and National Academy of Sciences India (2021), he has significantly advanced therapeutic strategies for immune disorders.

Prof. Dipyaman Ganguly

Ashoka University

Prof. Dipyaman Ganguly is a physician-scientist and Head of the Department of Biology at Ashoka University's Trivedi School of Biosciences, specializing in human immunology with a focus on plasmacytoid dendritic cells in autoreactive inflammation and mechanical cues in immune functions. Holding MBBS from Medical College Kolkata, PhDs in Biotechnology from Indian Institute of Chemical Biology and Immunology from UT MD Anderson Cancer Center, and postdoctoral experience at Columbia University, he returned to India in 2013 to lead research at CSIR-Indian Institute of Chemical Biology before joining Ashoka in 2024. His contributions include multi-omics studies on COVID-19 immune responses and awards like the Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize in Medical Sciences (2022), NASI-Scopus Young Scientist Award (2017), and fellowships from the Indian Academy of Sciences and Royal Society of Biology (2024).

Dr. Amit Awasthi

THSTI, Faridabad

Dr. Amit Awasthi is a Senior Professor at the Translational Health Science and Technology Institute (THSTI) in Faridabad, with expertise in T cell differentiation and cytokine signaling in autoimmunity, inflammation, and infections. Trained as an Instructor at Harvard Medical School and with postdoctoral work at Brigham and Women's Hospital, his research explores Th9, Th17, and regulatory T cells in diseases like multiple sclerosis, Crohn's disease, and COVID-19, alongside immunotherapy and vaccine development using nanoparticles. Notable achievements include the GN Ramachandran National Bioscience Award (2021), DBT-Wellcome India Alliance Intermediate Fellowship (2012), and an Indian patent for chitosan oligosaccharide nanoparticles (2020); he currently serves as President of the Indian Immunology Society.

Prof. Ramnath Mishra

KIMS, Bhubaneswar

Prof. Ramnath Misra is Professor Emeritus and former Head of the Department of Clinical Immunology and Rheumatology at Kalinga Institute of Medical Sciences (KIMS) in Bhubaneswar, renowned for his expertise in diagnosing and managing autoimmune and musculoskeletal disorders like systemic lupus erythematosus and rheumatic diseases. With an MBBS and MD from SCB Medical College, Cuttack, and FRCP from the UK, he has pioneered clinical immunology training and research in India, contributing to flow cytometry, autoantibodies, and inflammation studies. His accolades include the APLAR Masters Award (2021) and American College of Rheumatology Masters Award (2024), reflecting his vast impact on rheumatology education and patient care.

Dr. Bidyut Das

SCB Medical College, Cuttack

Dr. Bidyut Kumar Das is Professor and Head of the Department of Clinical Immunology and Rheumatology at SCB Medical College and Hospital in Cuttack, with over 40 years of experience in internal medicine, immunology, and rheumatology. An alumnus of SCB (MBBS 1979, MD 1984) and PhD in Immunology from Utkal University (2004), his research covers cytokine signatures in infectious diseases like malaria and SLE, genetic polymorphisms, and vitamin D's immunomodulatory role. Honored with awards like the Samanta Chandrasekhar Award (2018), Odisha Citizen’s Award (2012), and Pfizer Gold Medal (1979), he has transformed tele-rheumatology services, treating over 40,000 patients annually at no cost to the underprivileged.

Dr. Vineeta Shoba

St Johns Med College

Dr. Vineeta Shobha is Professor and Head of the Department of Clinical Immunology and Rheumatology at St. John's Medical College Hospital in Bengaluru, specializing in lupus, myositis, psoriasis, and COVID-19 impacts on autoimmune diseases. Holding MD and DM in Clinical Immunology and Rheumatology, she contributes to pan-India registries for lupus, scleroderma, and myositis, and serves as President of the Karnataka Rheumatology Association and Core Committee member for IRA Myositis and Lupus SIGs. Her work emphasizes assessment trends in systemic sclerosis and prescription practices across Indian healthcare settings.

Dr. Aman Sharma

PGI, Chandigarh

Dr. Aman Sharma is Professor in the Department of Internal Medicine (Clinical Immunology and Rheumatology) at PGIMER in Chandigarh, focusing on systemic vasculitis (e.g., AAV, DADA2), relapsing polychondritis, psoriatic arthritis, and extrapulmonary tuberculosis including HIV-TB coinfection. With MBBS and MD from PGIMER, plus FAMS, FIACM, FICP, FACR, and FRCP (London), he founded the Indian Vasculitis and Relapsing Polychondritis Registries in 2017 and leads international collaborations like GRAPPA. Awards include the Rabindranath Tagore Oration (2019), International ACR Fellowship (2015), and Zydus Oration (2011); he is President Elect of the Indian Rheumatology Association.
